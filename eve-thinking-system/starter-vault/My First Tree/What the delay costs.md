title: What the delay costs
---

Putting it off is never free — but I only ever count the obvious cost (the thing done late). What about the quieter costs: the hours it rents in the back of my mind, the worse version I produce when I finally rush it, the trust I lose in my own word? Which of those is actually the biggest, and why do I keep ignoring it?
