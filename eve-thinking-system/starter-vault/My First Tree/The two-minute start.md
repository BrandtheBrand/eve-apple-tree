title: The two-minute start
---

The smallest possible version of starting: do the first two minutes and let myself stop. Most of the resistance seems to live in *beginning*, not in *doing* — so what happens if I make beginning almost free?

First pass: the point isn't the two minutes, it's that starting badly beats not starting. Shipping the tiniest piece teaches me faster than one more plan.
