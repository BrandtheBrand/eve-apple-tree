# 🍎 Eve's Apple Tree Thinking System

Most note apps are filing cabinets: you put things in and they sit there flat. This one treats your notes as a **living tree of thought** and pairs you with **Eve's tree team (AI companion kit)**: a gardener, a challenger, and an observer whose only job is to make you think more, think wider, and never lose a good idea. As you think, the tree grows **taller** (over time) and **wider** (across fields and links), and you can fly around it in 3D. The point isn't organizing information; it's **growing a thinker**.

Eve's tree team tends the tree with you. It proposes leaves and connections; you decide what to keep. It never creates your ah-ha moments or your outputs, and it writes nothing without your approval.

> **Fastest start (beginners): [`starter-vault/`](starter-vault/)** — a ready-to-open Obsidian vault with the plugin pre-bundled, Eve's tree team installed, and a real "My First Tree" waiting inside. Open it, read `START-HERE.md`, and you're planting in three steps. Everything below is the deeper reference.

## The idea in one picture

- Every note is a **🍃 leaf** — one idea, titled as a short *handle* (a phrase or a raw question), not a finished thesis.
- Leaves grow in **fields** (lenses on how you think: *Researcher · Maker · Critic · Storyteller*) and rise over **time** (old at the bottom, new at the top).
- Connections are **branches** and, across fields, **rhizome** threads. The web of links *is* the structure. No rigid folders.
- A breakthrough is a **🌸 flower** (an *Ah-ha* you'll never lose); a finished thing is a **🍎 apple** (an output — you learn by producing).
- The deeper soil is your **🌱 root**: *how and why you think.*

## Your first 30 minutes

1. **See a tree first.** Open `viz/renaissance-demo.html` in a browser and fly around: scroll to zoom in (titles, then descriptions appear), pull back and the tree becomes a green silhouette in a forest. (Or, to see your own vault live, install the Obsidian plugin in `../plugin/`.)
2. **Give the team the contract.** Either drop `CLAUDE.md` into an Obsidian vault opened with Claude Code, or install the team as a skill (`agents/README.md`). Now the gardener knows how to work with you.
3. **Calibrate its voice.** Run the **DOT-VOICE** exercise: the gardener hands you one short source and you hand-write 3–5 dots in your own words. It titles like you from then on.
4. **Plant 3 dots.** Just talk — say three thoughts. The gardener turns each into a clean leaf, proposes a link, and hands you a question from a lens you skipped. You decide what to keep, and you bloom the first Ah-ha yourself.
5. **Look at your tree.** Render it and watch it stand up. Come back tomorrow and it's taller.
