# How to use Eve's Apple Tree Thinking System

A practical walkthrough for you, the human. The Gardener (your AI) does the formatting and the nudging — you just think out loud.

---

## Your first 10 minutes

1. **Plant the root.** Tell the Gardener, in a sentence or two, *how and why you think* — what you're curious about, how you like to work things out. It saves this as your 🌱 **root**. (Don't overthink it; it grows.)
2. **Name a topic.** Pick something you're chewing on. The Gardener starts a 🪵 **trunk** for it.
3. **Drop three thoughts.** Just say them. Each becomes a 🍃 **leaf**. Notice the Gardener doesn't make you format anything.
4. **Let it provoke you.** The Gardener will hand you a question from a lens you didn't use. Follow it. That's the whole game.
5. **Bloom once.** When something clicks — "oh, that's actually the same as…" — say so. The Gardener captures a 🌸 **flower**. You just made your first memory.

That's it. Come back tomorrow and the tree is taller.

---

## The six moves (what actually happens)

You don't need to memorize these — the Gardener runs them. But knowing them helps you play along.

| Move | You… | The Gardener… |
|---|---|---|
| 🌱 **Plant** | say a thought | turns it into a clean leaf |
| ✨ **Inspire** | feel stuck or curious | hands you the question / lens you're missing |
| 🕸 **Extend** | have related ideas | finds the cross-field link and proposes it |
| 🌸 **Bloom** | have an Ah-ha | captures it forever, brings it back later |
| 🍎 **Harvest** | have enough to say something | nudges you to *produce* a small output |
| 🍂 **Tend** | come back later | resurfaces old seeds, adds rings, composts the dead |

---

## The lenses (how to think wider)

Your fields are four ways of looking at any idea. The tree grows **wider** when you walk an idea past more than one:

- **Researcher** — *What's the evidence? What would change my mind?*
- **Maker** — *What's the smallest thing I could build to find out?*
- **Critic** — *What's the weakest link? Who pays?*
- **Storyteller** — *Who's it for? What's the one-sentence version?*

If you only ever think as a Maker, your tree leans. Let the Gardener pull you into the other three.

> You can rename or add fields — tell the Gardener your own lenses (e.g. *Strategist, Teacher, Investor*). They become new sectors of the tree.

---

## How the tree grows (and why that feels good)

- **Taller** = time. Every day you think, new leaves rise toward the top.
- **Wider** = breadth. More fields walked + more cross-field links = a fuller canopy.
- **Rings** = depth. Revisit an old note and understand it better — it thickens.
- **Flowers** = the keepers. Your Ah-ha moments, never lost.
- **Apples** = proof. The things you actually produced.

Open the 3D view (`../demo/index.html`, or the Obsidian plugin) now and then. Watching your own tree of light grow taller and wider is the point — the same satisfaction as growing a real tree, and a picture of your own learning.

---

## Good habits

- **Think out loud; don't format.** That's the Gardener's job.
- **Bloom generously.** When something clicks, say it. A captured Ah-ha is worth a hundred tidy notes.
- **Chase the link, not the folder.** The surprising cross-field connection is where the value is.
- **Harvest small.** Don't wait for the masterpiece. A 200-word apple completes the thought.
- **Tend, don't hoard.** Let old leaves resurface; let dead ones compost. A tended tree beats a stuffed cabinet.
