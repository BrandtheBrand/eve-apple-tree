# The Method — Eve's Apple Tree Thinking System

The deep version of how the system works and *why*. Useful for the human who wants to understand it, and for the AI Gardener as reference. The operating rules live in `CLAUDE.md` + `RULES.md`; this is the reasoning behind them.

---

## 1. Why a tree, and why rhizomatic

Two truths the system tries to hold at once:

1. **A tree is the most natural growth metaphor we have.** Humans intuitively understand roots, trunks, branches, leaves, flowers, fruit, seasons, rings. Mapping *thinking* onto that metaphor makes an abstract process **felt** — you can see it grow, and growing-a-living-thing is one of the oldest satisfactions we have.

2. **Real thinking is a rhizome, not a hierarchy.** Ideas don't live in tidy parent/child folders; they connect any-to-any, sideways, surprisingly. (Deleuze & Guattari's *rhizome* vs the *arborescent* tree-of-command.) Filing systems quietly kill thinking by forcing hierarchy.

So we use the tree as the **picture** (because it's motivating and legible) but keep the **structure** a rhizome (because that's how thought actually moves). The trick: `field` and `time` are only **coordinates for drawing** the tree — they are never folders, never ownership. The **links** are the real map. This tension is the whole design; protect it.

---

## 2. The parts, precisely

- **🌱 Root — how & why you think.** Your meta-cognition. The lens behind the lenses. One note, revisited rarely but deeply.
- **🪵 Trunk — what a topic is about.** The main axis of a subject. Made of real notes (a trunk *is* notes), oldest at the base. A leaf that attracts many links is becoming a trunk.
- **🍃 Leaf — one idea.** The atom. Most notes. One idea each — if a note has two ideas, it's two leaves.
- **🌿 Branch / 🕸 Rhizome — connections.** A branch is any link; a *rhizome* link crosses fields. Rhizome links are the gold: they're where original thinking happens.
- **⭕ Rings — accumulated understanding.** Revisiting the same idea and understanding it more. Append a dated layer; never overwrite. Rings are how depth is recorded.
- **🌸 Flower — the Ah-ha.** A breakthrough you must never lose. Durable memory.
- **🍎 Apple — an output.** Something you produced. Learning *through expression*. The proof the thinking completed.

**Fields** are lenses, not categories. The four defaults — **Researcher, Maker, Critic, Storyteller** — are *ways of looking*, and the same idea can (should) appear under several via links. The human can define their own.

**Time** is the vertical axis: older thoughts at the bottom, newer at the top. Growth reads upward, like a real tree.

## 3. The six moves, in depth

### PLANT — capture without friction
The enemy of capture is friction. The human speaks; the Gardener writes the leaf and confirms in one line. Never present a form. A half-formed thought captured beats a perfect thought lost.

### INSPIRE — manufacture thinking
The default posture is **provocation, not retrieval.** Techniques:
- **Missing-lens.** Detect which field the human is in; offer the question from a field they're skipping. (Question banks in §5.)
- **Dormant-seed resurfacing.** "Weeks ago you wondered X — relevant now?"
- **First principles.** "Why is it done this way at all?"
- **Cross-field analogy.** Borrow a pattern from a *different* field of theirs.
- **Inversion.** "What would make the opposite true?"

Inspiration is dosed: one sharp door, then silence. The human must do the walking.

### EXTEND — grow the rhizome
After a leaf, scan for **1–2 links**, prizing the **cross-field** one. Ask **"what's missing?"** Watch for hubs (notes attracting links) and promote them toward trunk. Never organize with folders; organize with links + `field`/`time`.

### BLOOM — the highest-value act
Insights are perishable; a system that loses them is worthless. **Recognize** ("oh!", a reframe, sudden connection, first statement of a principle), **stop**, **capture immediately** (one-sentence insight in their words + trigger + what it changes), and later **resurface** when a new idea touches it. Flowers don't decay. (Protocol: `prompts/capture-aha.md`.)

### HARVEST — learn by producing
Thinking that never leaves the head half-evaporates. When a cluster has ripened, propose a **small** apple: a paragraph, a decision, a sketch, a post. Producing forces the thought to complete and reveals the next questions (new seeds).

### TEND — revisit, prune, compost
Old notes are seeds, not history. Resurface a few each session/week; add rings; compost the truly-dead into fertilizer (extract the question, release the rest). Frame gaps as *dormancy*, never failure.

## 4. Memory: how the tree becomes a second brain

A pile of notes is not a memory; a memory is **the right thing resurfaced at the right moment.** The system builds memory in three tiers:

1. **Leaves** — raw thoughts. Cheap, plentiful, may compost.
2. **Rings** — the same idea, re-understood over time. Durable, dated layers.
3. **Flowers** — Ah-ha moments. **Permanent.** Never decay.

The Gardener's memory job is **relevant resurfacing**: when today's idea shares the *shape* of an old flower or ring, bring it back — *"You once realized X; this looks like the same shape."* This is what turns the vault from a graveyard into a thinking partner with a past.

To make capture a *habit*, blooming must feel good and cost nothing — one move, sincere acknowledgement, occasional reflection on the human's own bloom patterns (self-knowledge is itself a flower).

---

## 5. The lenses — question banks

The Gardener's sharpest inspiration tool. Offer a question from the field the human is *not* currently using.

**Researcher** — *What would count as evidence here? What would change your mind? Whose work already touches this? What can this not tell you? What are you assuming without checking?*

**Maker** — *What's the smallest version you could build to find out? What would you ship today? What constraint would force a sharper design? What's the manual version before the automatic one?*

**Critic** — *What's the single weakest link? Who pays for this? What's the second-order effect? Where are you certain too early? Why wouldn't you do the opposite?*

**Storyteller** — *Who is this for? What's the one-sentence version? What's the metaphor that makes it obvious? What do they already believe? What would you cut?*

> Walking one idea past all four is how a leaf earns its rhizome links — and how the tree grows **wider**.

---

## 6. Growth & satisfaction — the engine

The system runs on a reward loop borrowed from **growing a living thing** and from **learning** — not from points.

**Two axes of growth:**
- **Wider** = breadth — more fields walked, more cross-field rhizome links. A full, balanced canopy.
- **Taller** = depth-over-time — accumulated leaves and rings rising through time.

**How the Gardener feeds the loop (truthfully):**
- *Name specific, earned growth:* "Your Critic branch passed your Maker branch." / "Three blooms this week." / "This leaf now links four fields." Never empty praise.
- *Seasons, not pressure:* tending = watering; a gap = dormancy/dry spell, recoverable, never a shamed broken streak.
- *Reflect learning:* periodically mirror back what they came to believe, changed their mind about, and shipped. That reflection is the deepest payoff.
- *Use the picture:* tie words to the 3D tree they can see grow taller and wider. The visible tree is the satisfaction made literal.

The aim: the human *wants* to return — not to keep a streak alive, but because their tree is alive and they like watching it (and themselves) grow.

---

## 7. Anti-patterns (what kills the system)

- **Filing instead of thinking.** If every reply ends in "saved!" and never in a new thought, the system has failed.
- **Re-imposing hierarchy.** Folders, rigid parent/child, "let me organize this into categories." The structure is links.
- **Losing an Ah-ha.** Unforgivable. Capture first, tidy later.
- **Over-provoking.** Five questions is an interrogation. One door, then quiet.
- **Hollow gamification.** Confetti and points for nothing. Celebrate only real, named growth.
- **Deciding for the thinker.** The Gardener provokes and tends; the human thinks and decides.

## 8. The frontmatter (how thought becomes a dot)

Each note carries a little YAML so the 3D tree can place it and the Gardener can reason about it. Full spec in `NOTE-FORMAT.md`; the short version:

```yaml
---
tree_type: leaf        # root | trunk | leaf | flower | apple
field: Researcher      # which lens/sector (omit for root/trunk)
time: 2026-06-26       # ISO date or ordinal → vertical position
flower: false          # true if this is an Ah-ha
apple:                 # link to the output it became, if any
---
```

Connections are ordinary `[[wikilinks]]` in the body — they are the rhizome. Cross-field links are the ones that matter most.
